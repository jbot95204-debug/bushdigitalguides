Your Google Business Profile is often the first thing a local customer sees. If it is incomplete, inconsistent, or out of date, people may never reach your website.

Bush Digital Guides helps regional trade and service businesses improve the practical parts of their Google presence without changing anything without approval. The goal is not to chase tricks. It is to help nearby customers understand what you do, where you work, whether you look active, and how to contact you safely.

## What a good profile should make clear

A useful Google Business Profile should show:

- the real business name customers know
- accurate categories and services
- current opening hours or clear enquiry expectations
- service areas you genuinely cover
- a working phone number and website link
- recent project, vehicle, team, or site photos where appropriate
- reviews that reflect real customer experiences
- review responses that sound like the business
- a clear next step for quotes, bookings, or questions

For a Victor Harbor or Fleurieu Peninsula trade business, the profile should also connect cleanly with the website. If the profile says “roof repairs”, “landscaping”, “solar”, “air conditioning”, “pest control”, or another core service, the website should make that service easy to find and enquire about.

## Common Google Profile issues we look for

Many regional businesses have a profile that was set up years ago and then left alone. Common gaps include:

- old photos that no longer represent the business
- vague or missing service categories
- service areas that are too broad, too narrow, or unclear
- opening hours that do not match how enquiries are actually handled
- a website link that sends people to a confusing homepage
- no simple review request process for genuine customers
- unanswered reviews or replies that feel generic
- contact buttons that do not match the preferred enquiry path
- profile changes made by old agencies, ex-staff, or unknown accounts

These are not just marketing issues. They can affect customer trust, lead quality, and whether the owner knows who can change important business details online.

## A practical improvement path

A sensible Google Profile cleanup usually starts with a small checklist:

1. Confirm who owns and manages the profile.
2. Check the business name, categories, services, hours, phone, website link, and service area.
3. Compare the profile against the website so customers see the same services and locations in both places.
4. Add or plan real photos that show the current business, team, vehicles, jobs, or work areas.
5. Draft review response examples in the business's voice.
6. Set a simple, ethical process for asking genuine customers for honest feedback.
7. Make sure quote, call, and contact paths are easy on mobile.

Bush Digital Guides can help prepare the recommendations and draft updates, but profile edits, public replies, and customer messages should be approved by the business owner before they go live.

## Review requests should stay ethical

A healthy review process asks real customers for honest feedback at an appropriate time. It should not pressure people, offer incentives, or only ask people who seem likely to leave positive comments.

Useful review-system wording is simple: thank the customer, explain that feedback helps local people choose a business, and give them an easy way to leave a review if they want to. If feedback is negative or sensitive, the business should handle it carefully rather than automating a public response.

## Digital safety basics for Google profiles

A Google Business Profile is part of the business's digital front door. The safety basics are practical:

- know which Google accounts have owner or manager access
- remove old agency or staff access where appropriate
- use unique logins and two-factor authentication where supported
- make sure public phone, website, and hours are not changed without approval
- avoid giving profile access to unnecessary tools or contractors
- keep review and enquiry summaries away from unapproved personal inboxes
