## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is value-add property investment in Australia?

Value-add property investment means actively creating equity rather than waiting for market growth. Strategies include building granny flats, subdividing land, converting to dual occupancy, and renovating to increase rental yield. In Australia, these strategies can add $50,000–$300,000+ in equity depending on the project and location.

### How much does a granny flat cost to build in Australia in 2026?

Granny flat costs in Australia range from $80,000–$180,000 depending on size, finish, and state. A basic 60m² granny flat typically costs $100,000–$130,000. The book includes state-by-state build costs, approval requirements, and rental yield calculations for every capital city and major regional area.

### Is subdividing your property worth it in Australia?

Subdivision can be extremely profitable — typically adding $100,000–$400,000 in value — but it depends on your block size, zoning, council requirements, and local market. The book includes a feasibility calculator and three real case studies: one that made $280,000, one that broke even, and one that lost money. All three are instructive.

### What's the best value-add property strategy for beginners?

Granny flats are the lowest-risk entry point. They're the cheapest to build, easiest to get approved (especially in NSW and SA post-2024 reforms), and provide immediate rental income. The book recommends starting with a granny flat on your first or second investment property before moving to subdivisions or duplex builds.

### How do you find properties suitable for value-add strategies?

The book teaches a systematic approach: filter by zoning (R20+), minimum lot size, council planning scheme overlays, and infrastructure availability. It includes a 30-minute zoning research process that can save you $100,000 in mistakes, plus a due diligence checklist of 47 items to check before buying.

### What are the tax implications of value-add property in Australia?

Value-add strategies have significant tax implications including CGT (capital gains tax), GST margin scheme for subdivisions, depreciation on new builds, and land tax. The book covers all of these with worked examples, plus the proposed CGT discount changes that could affect investors from 2026-27 onwards.
