AI for Tradies

How Australian Tradies Can Use AI to Win More Jobs and Work Smarter

📖 45,000 words 📑 18 chapters ✍️ Bush Digital Guides

What’s Inside

AI isn’t coming for your job. But the tradie down the road who learns to use it? They might take your customers.

This book cuts through the hype and shows Australian tradies — sparkies, plumbers, chippies, painters, landscapers — exactly how to use AI tools to run a tighter operation. No computer science degree required. No $10,000 software subscriptions. Just practical tools you can set up between jobs.

Who It’s For

  • Solo tradies who are great at the work but drowning in admin
  • Trade business owners with 2-10 staff who need better systems
  • Apprentices who want to get ahead before they even finish their trade
  • Any tradie who’s curious about AI but doesn’t know where to start

What You’ll Learn

Part 1 — AI for Tradies (No BS): What AI actually is (in plain English), what it can and can’t do for trade businesses, and the 5 areas where it makes the biggest difference.

Part 2 — Winning More Jobs: AI-powered quoting that’s faster and more accurate, automated follow-ups that close more jobs, Google review management, and using ChatGPT to write quotes that actually win work.

Part 3 — Running the Business: Automated scheduling and dispatch, smart invoicing and payment chasing, inventory management, and AI-assisted compliance paperwork.

Part 4 — On the Tools: Using AI for fault diagnosis, photo documentation, safety checklists, and training. Plus voice-to-text for site notes that actually get written up.

Part 5 — Marketing Without the Cringe: AI-generated social media content, Google Business Profile optimisation, automated review requests, and building a website that ranks — all without hiring a marketing agency.

Part 6 — Future-Proofing: Where trade AI is heading, what to invest in now, and the skills that will separate the busy tradies from the broke ones.

45,000 words. 18 chapters. Written for tradies, not tech bros.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be good with computers to use AI tools?

No. If you can use a smartphone and send a text, you can use the tools in this book. We walk through everything step by step with screenshots. Most of the AI tools covered have simple apps — no coding or technical knowledge required.

What AI tools do you recommend for Australian tradies?

The book covers a range of tools depending on your needs: ChatGPT and Claude for writing quotes and emails, ServiceM8 and Tradify for job management with AI features, Xero for automated invoicing, and free tools like Google’s AI features for marketing. Everything is tested against Australian conditions and pricing.

Will AI replace tradies?

No. AI can’t wire a switchboard, fix a leaking pipe, or hang a door. What it can do is handle the admin that keeps you at your desk until 10pm — quoting, invoicing, scheduling, and customer follow-ups. The tradies who use AI will simply have more time for billable work.

How much does it cost to set up AI automation for a trade business?

Most of the tools in this book are free or under $50/month. A typical tradie automation stack (job management + invoicing + AI assistant) costs $100-200/month and saves 10-15 hours per week. The book includes a full cost-benefit analysis with real numbers from Australian trade businesses.

Is this book only for solo tradies or also for bigger operations?

Both. Part 2 and 3 are particularly relevant for solo operators, while Parts 3-5 scale up for businesses with staff. Whether you’re a one-man-band sparky or running a team of 10 plumbers, there are practical takeaways throughout.

5 Reasons Every Australian Tradie Should Learn AI in 2026

  1. Your competitors already are — Early adopters are quoting faster and winning more jobs
  2. Admin is eating your profits — The average tradie spends 15+ hours/week on non-billable work
  3. Customer expectations have changed — People expect instant quotes, online booking, and digital invoices
  4. It’s cheaper than hiring — AI tools that cost $50/month can replace tasks that would cost $25/hour for an admin assistant
  5. The skills transfer everywhere — Once you learn to automate one process, you’ll spot opportunities everywhere in your business
