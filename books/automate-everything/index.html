Frequently Asked Questions

What can I automate in my small business in Australia?

Almost everything that doesn’t require your physical presence or expertise. The biggest wins are: invoicing and payment chasing (saves 3-5 hrs/week), appointment scheduling (saves 2-4 hrs/week), customer follow-ups (saves 2-3 hrs/week), social media posting, and bookkeeping reconciliation. The book maps out the top 20 automation opportunities ranked by time saved vs setup effort.

What are the best automation tools for Australian small businesses?

The book reviews and compares: Xero (accounting), ServiceM8 and Tradify (job management), Zapier and Make (workflow automation), n8n (free self-hosted alternative), ChatGPT and Claude (AI assistants), and dozens of specialty tools. Every recommendation considers Australian pricing, tax compliance, and local support.

How much does business automation cost?

A basic automation stack costs $50-200/month: Xero ($25-65), a scheduling tool ($0-50), and Zapier or Make ($0-50). The book’s “Weekend Sprint” chapter shows how to set up a complete automation system in a single weekend. Most readers report saving 10-15 hours per week — that’s $500-750/week in recovered billable time at typical tradie rates.

Is AI actually useful for small businesses or just hype?

It’s genuinely useful — but only for specific tasks. AI excels at drafting emails, writing quotes, summarising documents, and answering common customer questions. It’s terrible at anything requiring judgment, nuance, or current pricing. The book is honest about what works and what’s still marketing hype, with real before/after examples from Australian businesses.

Can I automate my business without technical skills?

Yes. The tools in this book are designed for non-technical users. Xero, ServiceM8, and most scheduling tools have drag-and-drop interfaces. Even Zapier (the “glue” that connects apps) uses a visual builder. The book includes step-by-step screenshots for every setup.

5 Signs Your Small Business Needs Automation

  1. You’re chasing invoices after 9pm — Automated payment reminders recover 30% more overdue invoices
  2. You’ve double-booked yourself (again) — Online scheduling eliminates phone tag and diary conflicts
  3. Customers complain about slow responses — Auto-replies and AI chatbots handle the basics instantly
  4. You can’t take a holiday — Automated workflows keep the business running while you’re away
  5. You know what to charge but not what you earned — Automated bookkeeping gives you real-time visibility
