**How STCs work, what you'll save, and why timing matters**

## What Are STCs?

Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs) are the Australian government's solar rebate mechanism. When you install solar panels or batteries, the system generates a number of "certificates" based on its size and location. These certificates have a dollar value, and your installer typically discounts them from your quote upfront.

**Translation:** STCs = the reason a $12,000 solar system costs you $8,000. The "rebate" isn't a cheque from the government ‚Äî it's a market-based discount applied at point of sale.

---

## How Many STCs Does Your System Generate?

### Panel STCs Formula

**STCs = System Size (kW) √ó Zone Rating √ó Deeming Years**

That's it. Three numbers multiplied together.

### Zone Ratings (How Sunny Is Your Area?)

Australia is divided into four zones based on solar radiation. Sunnier areas generate more STCs per kilowatt.

| Zone | Rating | Locations |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Zone 1** | 1.622 | Darwin, Far North QLD, Top End |
| **Zone 2** | 1.536 | Brisbane, Perth, most of QLD & WA |
| **Zone 3** | 1.382 | **Adelaide**, Sydney, Melbourne, Canberra, Hobart, most of SA/NSW/VIC |
| **Zone 4** | 1.185 | Parts of southern TAS, alpine regions |

*Most Australians are in Zone 2 or Zone 3.*

### Deeming Years (The Countdown)

STCs are calculated based on years remaining until 2030, when the scheme ends. This number drops every 1 January:

| Install Year | Deeming Years | Impact |
|-------------|--------------|--------|
| **2026** | **5** | **Current** |
| 2027 | 4 | 20% fewer STCs |
| 2028 | 3 | 40% fewer STCs |
| 2029 | 2 | 60% fewer STCs |
| 2030 | 1 | Last year ‚Äî minimum STCs |

**Key takeaway:** Every year you wait, your rebate drops by roughly 20%. The scheme doesn't end with a bang ‚Äî it fades out.

## Battery STCs ‚Äî The New Kid

Since April 2023, eligible battery systems also generate STCs. This is separate from the panel STCs.

### Battery STC Formula

**Battery STCs = Usable Capacity (kWh) √ó STC Factor**

| Period | STC Factor per kWh | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| Until 30 April 2026 | **8.4 STCs/kWh** | Current rate |
| From 1 May 2026 | **Lower rate (TBC)** | Rate reduces ‚Äî install before May to maximise |

**Important limits:**
- Only the first **50 kWh of usable capacity** qualifies
- Battery must be on the CEC Approved Batteries list
- "Usable" capacity is typically 90% of nominal capacity

**On a 10kWh battery, this could mean $1,000+ difference in your rebate.**

## Tips to Maximise Your Rebate

1. **Don't wait.** Deeming years drop every January. A 2026 install gets 5 years; a 2027 install gets 4. That's a 20% reduction.

2. **Install batteries before 1 May 2026.** The STC factor drops after this date. If you're on the fence, this is your deadline.

3. **Use a CEC-accredited installer.** STCs can only be created by Clean Energy Council accredited installers. No accreditation = no rebate.

4. **Check your battery is on the CEC list.** Not all batteries qualify. Your installer should confirm this, but it's worth checking yourself at the CEC website.

5. **Get 3 quotes minimum.** STC values are standardised, but installer margins vary wildly. The same system can differ by $3,000+ between quotes.

6. **Ask how STCs are handled.** Most installers discount STCs upfront (you "assign" them to the installer). Some offer cash-back instead. Either way, make sure the STC value is clearly shown on your quote.
