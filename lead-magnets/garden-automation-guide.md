### Setup Steps

1. **Screw the nut and tail onto your garden tap.** Hand-tight is fine.
2. **Attach the pressure reducer/filter.** This stops your drippers blowing off ‚Äî Aussie mains pressure (300‚Äì500 kPa) will destroy drip fittings without it.
3. **Connect the tap timer** to the reducer outlet.
4. **Lay your poly pipe** along the garden bed edges. Tip: leave it in the sun for 20 minutes first ‚Äî it'll soften and be much easier to work with.
5. **Add fittings:** Tees for branches, end plugs to cap each run. Push in firmly, secure with clamps.
6. **Punch holes and insert drippers** at each plant. Use a 4mm hole punch ($3) or a nail. Twist the dripper cap to adjust flow ‚Äî more for tomatoes, less for herbs.
7. **Program the timer:** Early morning (5‚Äì6am), 15‚Äì20 minutes, every second day.
8. **Bury under mulch.** Aussie UV will make exposed poly pipe brittle within a year.

**Done.** Your garden waters itself.

## Home Assistant Basics (For the Curious)

Home Assistant is a free, open-source home automation platform that runs on a small computer in your house. It connects your sensors, controllers, weather data, and valves into one intelligent system.

### Why Bother?

- **Local control** ‚Äî works without internet
- **Combines everything** ‚Äî BOM weather + soil sensors + irrigation controller + water restrictions
- **Automations like:** "Only water at sunrise if soil moisture is below 40% AND no rain is forecast today"

### Key Integrations for Garden Automation

- **Bureau of Meteorology** ‚Äî Free weather data, rain forecasts, temperature
- **Irrigation Unlimited** (HACS) ‚Äî Multi-zone scheduling with water restriction compliance. Built by an Aussie.
- **Smart Irrigation** (HACS) ‚Äî Evapotranspiration-based "should I water today?" calculations
- **ESPHome** ‚Äî Build your own sensors for ~$20 each

### The Honest Bit

Home Assistant has a learning curve that's less "curve" and more "cliff face with handholds." It'll take several weekends to set up properly. Things will break. But the community is enormous and genuinely helpful ‚Äî and once it's running, it's the most powerful garden automation platform available.

## Water Restriction Compliance

Most Australian states have permanent or seasonal watering schedules. Smart controllers handle this automatically, but make sure you configure them:

| State | General Rules |
|-------|--------------|
| **SA** | Sprinklers before 10am or after 5pm, alternate days. Drip anytime. |
| **VIC** | Permanent water-saving rules. During restrictions: alternate days, 6‚Äì10am and 6‚Äì10pm. |
| **NSW** | Before 10am or after 4pm. |
| **QLD (SEQ)** | Odd/even house numbers on alternate days. |

**Key insight: Drip irrigation is generally exempt from most restriction levels.** Even when sprinklers are banned, drip usually remains permitted. Design your system around drip and you're restriction-proof.

## Your Weekend Action Plan

### Saturday Morning (1‚Äì2 hours)

1. Walk your garden. Decide what needs water and how often.
2. Drive to Bunnings. Buy the B-Hyve (or your chosen controller) + drip irrigation supplies.
3. Install the controller. Mount near a power outlet with WiFi signal.
4. Run your drip lines. Lay Techline PC tube along garden beds, connect with tees and elbows, cap with end plugs.
5. Download the app. Set up zones and schedules.

### Saturday Arvo (30 minutes)

6. Test every zone. Walk around checking for leaks and dry spots.
7. Set your schedule: 6am, 15‚Äì20 minutes per zone, every second day.
8. Enable weather-responsive mode in the app.

### Sunday (optional, for keen types)

9. Order an Ecowitt WH51 soil sensor from Amazon.
10. When it arrives, stick it in your most important bed at root depth (100‚Äì150mm).
11. Set a moisture threshold so the system skips watering when the soil's already wet.

**That's it.** Your garden now waters itself, checks the weather, and knows when the soil is dry. Go sit on the couch.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå **Skipping the pressure reducer** ‚Äî Aussie mains pressure will blow drip fittings apart. Always use one.

‚ùå **Leaving poly pipe in direct sun** ‚Äî UV destroys it within a season. Bury under mulch.

‚ùå **Forgetting the inline filter** ‚Äî Sediment blocks drippers. Clean the filter screen every few months.

‚ùå **Overwatering** ‚Äî More plants die from overwatering than underwatering. Start with less and increase if plants look stressed.

‚ùå **Dead batteries in tap timers** ‚Äî Set a calendar reminder every 3‚Äì4 months. Dead batteries = dead garden (valve fails closed).

‚ùå **Buying the cheapest WiFi timer** ‚Äî The $99 Holman WX1 has consistent reliability complaints. Spend the extra $160 on the B-Hyve and save yourself the headache.
